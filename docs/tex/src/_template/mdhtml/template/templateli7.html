You can define your equation by utilizing equation environment. Thus, the code 

\begin{equation}
    \label{eqn_sysmarkov}
    \begin{aligned}
        & \State_{t+1} \sim \transit(\bullet \vert \state_t, \action_t)
    \end{aligned}
\end{equation}

provides us \begin {equation} \label {eqn_sysmarkov} \begin {aligned} & \State _{t+1} \sim \transit (\bullet \vert \state _t, \action _t) \end {aligned} \end {equation}

The dollar sign symbol $ is also supported for denoting equations. For instance, writing $x ^ 2$ will yield the mathematical expression \(x ^ 2\).
